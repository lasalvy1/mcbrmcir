  • Potentially reduced risk charge

    End of 2015 EIOPA, the European Insurance and Occupational Pension Authority, was asked by the European Commission for potential specific treatment of infrastructure investments under Solvency II. This includes the definition of certain characteristics for infrastructure investments in order to be eligible for a reduced risk charge under the standard formula. The process to finalize the detailed requirements is still ongoing.

    Respectively, infrastructure investments satisfying the (still to be finalized) EIOPA requirements could be quite beneficial for insurers from a risk capital perspective going forward. This view is even more supported, considering that capital treatment for competing investors like banks is not that beneficial. Nevertheless, in order to benefit from special capital treatment, it is of key importance for insurers to understand the regulatory requirements in detail, and additionally be able to source a good amount of potentially qualifying infrastructure deals.

  • Utilization of a matching adjustment

    The so called “matching adjustment” enables insurers to reduce their liabilities, if there is a “proper” match of certain assets and liabilities on their balance sheet. Under Solvency II specific eligibility criteria exist (CF characteristics, ongoing portfolio management, governance, ..). These need to be fulfilled in order to apply the matching adjustment in the calculation of insurance liabilities. Insurers with long-term liabilities respectively can benefit from long-term investments, e.g. in infrastructure assets. In case they are eligible for the matching adjustment, insurance liabilities can be calculated using higher discount rates (including the so-called “matching adjustment”). The higher discount rate results in lower liabilities and strengthening of the overall balance sheet.

  • India’s Potential – Some Facts & Figures

    India is not India. With a population of 1.3 billion spread across 29 states and 100+ major languages, India has to be considered more as a continent, than as a country. Despite the rich culture and diversity of the country, from an economic perspective, India has the important boxes ticked in order to live up to the expectation of being the global growth engine of the next decades. India is blessed with a large, young and skilled population, 65% being under the age of 35 years, as well as enormous and robust domestic demand. Combined with the massive workforce, also the lacking infrastructure in certain parts, which needs to be ramped up quickly, can serve as a further growth driver instead of being a handicap. Married with the right long-term Government initiatives the baseline for India’s sustainable and unrivaled growth story is set.

  • The status-quo – a challenging environment for insurance companies

    In today’s financial markets insurance companies, especially life insurers, are facing challenging times. The long-term nature of their business and the general low and nowadays even negative yield environment for Government bonds puts life insurers under pressure to seek out for alternative investment options to generate the guaranteed rates to policyholders. Additional pressure from the regulatory side and FinTech competitors makes it even more challenging to fulfill obligations to policyholders while keeping up with shareholder expectations.
